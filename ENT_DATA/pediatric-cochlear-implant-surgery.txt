Indications of Pediatric Cochlear Implant Surgery
Pediatric Cochlear Implant Surgery is recommended for children with severe to profound hearing loss who do not benefit from traditional hearing aids.
Have congenital hearing loss
Have acquired hearing loss due to illness or injury
Have progressive hearing loss that cannot be corrected with other treatments
Before undergoing Pediatric Cochlear Implant Surgery, your child will undergo a thorough evaluation to determine if they are a suitable candidate for the procedure.
Our team of specialists will assess your child's hearing loss, overall health, and communication abilities to ensure that cochlear implant surgery is the best treatment option for them.
Steps involved in Pediatric Cochlear Implant Surgery Surgery
Pediatric Cochlear Implant Surgery is a multi-step process that involves several key stages:
Evaluation: Your child will undergo a series of tests and assessments to determine their candidacy for cochlear implant surgery.
Surgery: The cochlear implant device will be surgically implanted under the skin behind the ear, with electrodes placed in the cochlea to stimulate the auditory nerve.
Activation: After the surgery, the implant will be activated, and your child will begin the process of adjusting to hearing with the device.
Rehabilitation: Your child will work with a team of audiologists and speech therapists to develop listening and communication skills with the cochlear implant.

Frequently Asked Questions
What is pediatric cochlear implant surgery?
Pediatric cochlear implant surgery is a procedure where a device is implanted in a child's ear to help improve hearing by stimulating the auditory nerve.
How is cochlear implant surgery performed on children?
Cochlear implant surgery in children involves making a small incision behind the ear, placing the implant under the skin, and threading electrodes into the cochlea to stimulate the hearing nerve.
What are the benefits of cochlear implants for children?
Cochlear implants can help children with severe hearing loss develop speech and language skills, improve their ability to communicate, and enhance their quality of life.
How long does it take for children to benefit from cochlear implants?
Children can start benefiting from cochlear implants soon after the device is activated, but it may take several months of practice and therapy to maximize their hearing potential.
What age is ideal for pediatric cochlear implant surgery?
Pediatric cochlear implant surgery is typically recommended between 12 and 18 months of age to optimize language development.
How successful are cochlear implants in children?
Cochlear implants are highly successful in children with hearing loss, helping them develop speech and language skills similar to their peers with normal hearing.
What is the recovery time for pediatric cochlear implant surgery?
Recovery time for pediatric cochlear implant surgery is typically around 2 to 4 weeks. Your child may need time to heal and adjust after the procedure.
Can a child have both hearing aids and cochlear implants?
Yes, some children may benefit from using both hearing aids and cochlear implants simultaneously to maximize their hearing abilities.
Are cochlear implants covered by insurance for children?
In many cases, insurance plans cover cochlear implants for children as they are considered essential for hearing improvement. It's best to check with your insurance provider to confirm coverage.
What should parents expect during the process of getting a cochlear implant for their child?
During the process of getting a cochlear implant for their child, parents can expect multiple appointments for evaluation, surgery to implant the device, a period of healing, and follow-up visits for programming and therapy.
