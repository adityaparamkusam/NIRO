Laryngospasm: Diagnosis and Treatment
Laryngospasm is a sudden, involuntary closure of the vocal cords that can make it hard to breathe or speak. It often happens after surgery or in response to irritants like stomach acid. While usually brief, severe cases can be life-threatening. Laryngospasm is more common in children and people with certain medical conditions. If you experience difficulty breathing or a feeling of throat tightness, seek immediate medical help. Understanding laryngospasm can help you recognize and manage this condition effectively.
Causes of Laryngospasm
Laryngospasm can be triggered by various factors, leading to the sudden closure of the vocal cords. Some main contributors to its development include:
Gastroesophageal reflux disease (GERD)
Inhaled irritants or allergens
Postoperative complications
Exposure to cold air or water
Emotional stress or anxiety
Symptoms of Laryngospasm
Recognizing the symptoms of Laryngospasm early on is crucial as it can significantly impact outcomes and lead to prompt intervention. Symptoms of Laryngospasm to watch out for include:
Sudden inability to speak or breathe
Noisy breathing or
stridor
Chest tightness or wheezing
Panic or anxiety
Blue discoloration of the skin (cyanosis)
Early detection of these symptoms can help in quickly addressing the issue and preventing further complications. If you or someone experiences these symptoms, seek medical attention promptly.
Diagnosis of Laryngospasm
Diagnosing Laryngospasm is crucial for proper treatment and preventing complications. It involves a thorough evaluation by an ENT specialist. Symptoms like sudden choking or difficulty breathing are key indicators. Diagnostic methods may include:
Physical examination of the throat and vocal cords
Laryngoscopy to visualize the larynx
Pulmonary function tests to assess breathing
Discussing medical history and possible triggers
X-rays or CT scans to rule out other issues

Prevention and Management of Laryngospasm
When it comes to preventing or managing Laryngospasm, lifestyle changes and proactive measures play a crucial role in maintaining overall throat health. Here are some key strategies to consider:
Lifestyle Modifications:
Avoiding triggers such as acid reflux, smoking, and irritants like strong chemicals or fumes.
Staying well-hydrated by drinking plenty of water throughout the day.
Eating a balanced diet rich in fruits, vegetables, and whole grains to support immune function.
Regular Screenings:
Regular visits to an ENT specialist for check-ups and early detection of any potential issues.
Monitoring any changes in voice, breathing, or swallowing patterns for prompt intervention.
Discussing any concerns or symptoms with a healthcare provider for appropriate management.
Supportive Care:
Practicing stress-reducing techniques like deep breathing, meditation, or yoga to relax the throat muscles.
Avoiding shouting or straining the voice to prevent unnecessary strain on the vocal cords.
Using a humidifier to keep the air moist and prevent irritation in the throat.

What is laryngospasm?
Laryngospasm is a sudden spasm of the vocal cords causing breathing difficulties. It can be triggered by irritants or reflux.
What triggers a laryngospasm episode?
Laryngospasm episodes can be triggered by irritants like gastric reflux, allergies, or inhaled substances.
How long does a laryngospasm last?
A laryngospasm typically lasts for a few seconds to a couple of minutes. It is a sudden, involuntary constriction of the vocal cords.
What are the symptoms of laryngospasm?
Symptoms of laryngospasm include sudden choking, difficulty breathing, and a feeling of tightness in the throat. Prompt medical attention is crucial.
Can laryngospasm be treated at home?
Laryngospasm can be managed at home by staying calm, sipping water, and focusing on slow, deep breaths to help relax the vocal cords.
What medications help with laryngospasm?
Medications like benzodiazepines and muscle relaxants can help manage laryngospasm effectively.
How is laryngospasm diagnosed?
ENT specialists diagnose laryngospasm through a physical exam, discussing symptoms, and may use laryngoscopy or bronchoscopy for a closer look.
Is laryngospasm dangerous?
Yes, laryngospasm can be dangerous as it causes a sudden closure of the vocal cords, leading to breathing difficulties. Prompt medical attention is crucial.
How can laryngospasm be prevented?
Prevent laryngospasm by avoiding triggers like acid reflux, staying hydrated, managing stress, and maintaining good vocal hygiene.
Can stress or anxiety cause laryngospasm?
Stress and anxiety can trigger laryngospasm by causing the vocal cords to spasm, leading to breathing difficulties.
