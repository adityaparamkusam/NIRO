Indications of Vocal Cord Polyp Removal
Vocal cord polyps are small, noncancerous growths that can develop on one or both vocal cords. These polyps can cause a variety of symptoms, including hoarseness, a
breathy voice
, and
vocal fatigue
.
If you are experiencing these symptoms, it may be an indication that you need vocal cord polyp removal surgery.

Steps involved in Vocal Cord Polyp Removal Surgery
Vocal cord polyp removal surgery is a relatively straightforward procedure that is typically performed under general anesthesia. During the surgery, the surgeon will use a laryngoscope to visualize the vocal cords and remove the polyps using specialized instruments.
The entire procedure usually takes less than an hour to complete.
After the surgery, you may experience some mild discomfort in your throat and voice changes. However, these symptoms are temporary and should improve as you heal.
Your surgeon will provide you with specific post-operative instructions to help facilitate a smooth recovery process.
Who will perform Vocal Cord Polyp Removal Surgery?
Vocal cord polyp removal surgery is typically performed by an otolaryngologist, also known as an ear, nose, and throat (ENT) surgeon.
These specialists have extensive training and experience in diagnosing and treating disorders of the head and neck, including vocal cord polyps.

Preparing for Vocal Cord Polyp Removal Surgery
Before undergoing vocal cord polyp removal surgery, your surgeon will provide you with specific instructions on how to prepare for the procedure. This may include avoiding certain medications, fasting before surgery, and arranging for someone to drive you home afterwards.
It is important to follow your surgeon's pre-operative instructions carefully to ensure a successful outcome. If you have any questions or concerns about the surgery or the preparation process, do not hesitate to contact your healthcare provider for clarification.
Recovery after Vocal Cord Polyp Removal surgery
Recovery after vocal cord polyp removal surgery is typically quick and relatively painless. Most patients can resume normal activities within a few days of the procedure.
However, it is important to follow your surgeon's post-operative instructions to ensure a smooth recovery process.
During the recovery period, you may experience some temporary voice changes and mild discomfort in your throat. These symptoms should improve as you heal.
Your surgeon may recommend
voice therapy
to help you regain strength and flexibility in your vocal cords.

Frequently Asked Questions
What is vocal cord polyp removal?
Vocal cord polyp removal is a procedure to surgically remove a noncancerous growth on the vocal cords that can affect voice quality. It is done to improve voice function and quality.
How is vocal cord polyp removal performed?
Vocal cord polyp removal is usually done as an outpatient procedure under general anesthesia, using a laryngoscope to visualize and remove the polyp with microsurgical instruments.
Why is vocal cord polyp removal necessary?
Vocal cord polyp removal is done to improve voice quality, reduce hoarseness, and prevent further growth or complications.
What are the risks of vocal cord polyp removal?
The risks of vocal cord polyp removal include bleeding, infection, scarring, changes in voice quality, and rare complications like nerve damage. Your doctor will discuss these risks with you before the procedure.
How long does recovery take after vocal cord polyp removal?
Recovery after vocal cord polyp removal typically takes about 1 to 2 weeks. Your doctor will provide specific instructions based on your individual situation.
Is vocal cord polyp removal effective for improving voice quality?
Yes, vocal cord polyp removal can be effective in improving voice quality by restoring normal function to the vocal cords.
How successful is vocal cord polyp removal in restoring voice function?
Vocal cord polyp removal is generally successful in restoring voice function, especially when the polyp is causing voice changes or difficulties. However, the success can vary based on individual factors and the extent of the polyp.
Are there any long-term side effects of vocal cord polyp removal?
Long-term side effects of vocal cord polyp removal are rare, but there is a small risk of changes in voice quality or recurring polyps. Your doctor can discuss specific risks based on your individual case.
Can vocal cord polyp removal help prevent further voice problems?
Yes, removing vocal cord polyps can help improve voice quality and reduce the risk of future voice problems.
Is vocal cord polyp removal covered by insurance?
Yes, vocal cord polyp removal is usually covered by insurance if it is deemed medically necessary by your healthcare provider.
