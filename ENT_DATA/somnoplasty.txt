Somnoplasty: Indications and Recovery
Somnoplasty is a minimally invasive surgical procedure that uses radiofrequency energy to reduce tissue in the soft palate and throat, effectively treating
snoring
and sleep apnea.
This innovative treatment is a popular choice for those looking for a non-surgical option to improve their quality of sleep and overall health.
Indications of Somnoplasty
Somnoplasty is recommended for individuals who suffer from chronic snoring, sleep apnea, or other sleep disorders caused by excess tissue in the throat.
It is a safe and effective treatment that can provide long-lasting relief for those struggling to get a good night's rest.

Who will perform Somnoplasty Surgery?
Somnoplasty surgery is typically performed by an ear, nose, and throat (ENT) specialist or a sleep medicine physician with experience in minimally invasive procedures. These healthcare professionals have the expertise and training needed to safely and effectively perform somnoplasty surgery.
Preparing for Somnoplasty Surgery
Before undergoing somnoplasty surgery, patients will need to follow specific preparation guidelines provided by their healthcare provider. This may include avoiding certain medications, fasting before the procedure, and arranging for transportation to and from the surgical facility.
Recovery after Somnoplasty surgery
Recovery after somnoplasty surgery is typically quick and comfortable for most patients. It is normal to experience some mild discomfort or swelling in the throat, but this should subside within a few days.
Patients are usually advised to rest and avoid strenuous activities during the initial recovery period.
Before and After Somnoplasty surgery
Before somnoplasty surgery, patients should discuss any concerns or questions with their healthcare provider to ensure they are well-informed about the procedure.
After surgery, patients can expect to see a significant reduction in snoring and improvement in sleep quality, leading to a better overall quality of life.

Frequently Asked Questions
What is somnoplasty surgery?
Somnoplasty surgery is a procedure that uses radiofrequency energy to shrink tissues in the back of the throat to treat snoring and sleep apnea. It aims to reduce airway obstruction during sleep.
When is somnoplasty recommended for snoring?
Somnoplasty is recommended for snoring when other conservative treatments like lifestyle changes or oral devices have not been effective in reducing snoring.
How does somnoplasty work?
Somnoplasty uses radiofrequency energy to shrink excess tissue in the back of the throat, reducing snoring and symptoms of sleep apnea.
What are the risks of somnoplasty?
The risks of somnoplasty may include temporary pain, swelling, bleeding, infection, or changes in voice. These side effects are usually mild and improve with time.
How long does recovery take after somnoplasty?
Recovery after somnoplasty typically takes about 1 to 2 weeks.
Is somnoplasty effective in treating snoring and sleep apnea?
Somnoplasty is a minimally invasive procedure that can be effective in reducing snoring but may not be as effective for sleep apnea. It's essential to consult with a healthcare provider to determine the best treatment options for your specific condition.
How long does the somnoplasty procedure take?
The somnoplasty procedure typically takes about 30-45 minutes to complete.
Is somnoplasty a minimally invasive procedure?
Yes, somnoplasty is a minimally invasive procedure used to treat snoring and mild to moderate obstructive sleep apnea.
How soon can I return to normal activities after somnoplasty?
You can usually return to normal activities within a few days after somnoplasty. It's important to follow your doctor's recommendations for a smooth recovery.
What follow-up care is needed after somnoplasty?
After somnoplasty, you may need to follow up with your doctor to monitor your progress and ensure proper healing. They will advise you on any specific care instructions and may recommend a sleep study to evaluate the effectiveness of the procedure.
