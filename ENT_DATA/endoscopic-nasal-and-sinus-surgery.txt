Endoscopic Nasal and Sinus Surgery: Indications and Recovery
Endoscopic Nasal and Sinus Surgery is a minimally invasive procedure used to treat
chronic sinusitis
,
nasal obstruction
, and other sinus-related issues.
This surgery is performed using an endoscope, a thin, flexible tube with a camera attached to it, allowing the surgeon to see inside the nasal and sinus cavities without making large incisions.
The goal of Endoscopic Nasal and Sinus Surgery is to open up blocked sinus passages, remove any obstructions, and improve overall sinus function.
This procedure can provide long-lasting relief for patients suffering from chronic sinusitis and
nasal congestion
, allowing them to breathe more easily and reduce symptoms such as headaches, facial pain, and
nasal discharge
.
Indications of Endoscopic Nasal and Sinus Surgery
Endoscopic Nasal and Sinus Surgery is recommended for patients who have not responded well to conservative treatments such as medications, nasal sprays, and sinus rinses.
Chronic sinusitis that does not improve with medication
Recurrent
sinus infections
nasal polyps
deviated septum
causing nasal obstruction
Tumors or growths in the nasal or sinus cavities
Your ENT specialist will evaluate your symptoms and medical history to determine if Endoscopic Nasal and Sinus Surgery is the right treatment option for you.
Steps involved in Endoscopic Nasal and Sinus Surgery Surgery
Endoscopic Nasal and Sinus Surgery is typically performed under general anesthesia on an outpatient basis, meaning you can go home the same day.
The surgeon inserts the endoscope into the nostrils to visualize the nasal and sinus cavities.
Using small surgical instruments, the surgeon removes any blockages or obstructions in the sinuses.
The surgeon may also straighten a deviated septum or remove nasal polyps if necessary.
Finally, the surgeon closes the incisions with dissolvable stitches and places nasal packing to support the healing process.
The entire procedure usually takes about 1-2 hours, depending on the complexity of the case.
Who will perform Endoscopic Nasal and Sinus Surgery Surgery?
Endoscopic Nasal and Sinus Surgery is performed by an Otolaryngologist, also known as an Ear, Nose, and Throat (ENT) specialist. These highly trained physicians have expertise in diagnosing and treating conditions affecting the ears, nose, and throat, including sinus issues.
Your ENT specialist will work closely with you to develop a personalized treatment plan that addresses your specific needs and concerns.
They will guide you through the entire surgical process, from pre-operative preparation to post-operative care, ensuring you have a smooth and successful recovery.
Preparing for Endoscopic Nasal and Sinus Surgery Surgery
Before undergoing Endoscopic Nasal and Sinus Surgery, your ENT specialist will provide you with detailed instructions on how to prepare for the procedure.
Stopping certain medications that can increase the risk of bleeding
Avoiding food and drink for a certain period before the surgery
Arranging for someone to drive you home after the procedure
Packing a bag with essential items for your recovery, such as medications and comfortable clothing
It's essential to follow your doctor's pre-operative instructions closely to ensure the surgery goes smoothly and you have the best possible outcome.
Recovery after Endoscopic Nasal and Sinus Surgery surgery
Recovery after Endoscopic Nasal and Sinus Surgery varies from patient to patient but typically involves some common experiences.
Mild pain or discomfort in the nasal area
Swelling and bruising around the eyes
Nasal congestion and drainage
Fatigue or drowsiness from the anesthesia
Your ENT specialist will provide you with detailed post-operative instructions on how to care for yourself at home. It's crucial to follow these guidelines closely to promote healing and reduce the risk of complications.
If you have any concerns or questions during your recovery, don't hesitate to contact your doctor for guidance.
Before and After Endoscopic Nasal and Sinus Surgery surgery

chronic sinusitis symptoms

Endoscopic Nasal and Sinus Surgery can be a life-changing procedure for patients suffering from chronic sinus issues. By addressing the underlying causes of your symptoms, this surgery can provide long-lasting relief and improve your quality of life.
If you're considering Endoscopic Nasal and Sinus Surgery, talk to your ENT specialist to learn more about the benefits and risks associated with this procedure.
Overall, Endoscopic Nasal and Sinus Surgery is a safe and effective treatment option for individuals with chronic sinusitis and nasal obstruction.
By working closely with your ENT specialist and following their guidance, you can achieve optimal results and enjoy a healthier, happier life free from sinus-related symptoms.
If you have any questions or concerns about Endoscopic Nasal and Sinus Surgery, don't hesitate to reach out to your healthcare provider for personalized advice and support.

Frequently Asked Questions
What is endoscopic nasal and sinus surgery?
Endoscopic nasal and sinus surgery is a minimally invasive procedure where a thin, flexible tube with a camera (endoscope) is used to visualize and treat issues in the nose and sinuses, such as polyps, blockages, or infections.
How is endoscopic nasal and sinus surgery performed?
Endoscopic nasal and sinus surgery is performed using a thin, flexible tube with a camera and light on the end (endoscope) inserted through the nostrils to view and treat nasal and sinus conditions.
What conditions can endoscopic nasal and sinus surgery treat?
Endoscopic nasal and sinus surgery can treat conditions such as chronic sinusitis, nasal polyps, deviated septum, and sinus tumors.
How long is recovery after endoscopic nasal and sinus surgery?
Recovery after endoscopic nasal and sinus surgery typically takes about 1-2 weeks, but it can vary depending on the individual and the extent of the procedure.
What are the risks associated with endoscopic nasal and sinus surgery?
The risks associated with endoscopic nasal and sinus surgery can include bleeding, infection, damage to surrounding structures, and potential need for additional surgeries.
How effective is endoscopic nasal and sinus surgery for chronic sinusitis?
Endoscopic nasal and sinus surgery is effective for chronic sinusitis when medications fail to provide relief. It can help improve symptoms and quality of life for many patients.
Is endoscopic nasal and sinus surgery a minimally invasive option?
Yes, endoscopic nasal and sinus surgery is a minimally invasive procedure that uses a thin, flexible tube with a camera to visualize and treat nasal and sinus conditions.
What should I expect during recovery from endoscopic nasal and sinus surgery?
After endoscopic nasal and sinus surgery, you may experience mild discomfort, nasal congestion, and mild bleeding for a few days. It's essential to follow your doctor's instructions for care and attend follow-up appointments for a smooth recovery.
How can I prepare for endoscopic nasal and sinus surgery?
Before your endoscopic nasal and sinus surgery, follow your doctor's instructions on fasting, medications, and any other specific requirements. Be sure to discuss any concerns or questions with your healthcare provider before the procedure.
Can endoscopic nasal and sinus surgery improve breathing?
Yes, endoscopic nasal and sinus surgery can help improve breathing by correcting issues such as a deviated septum, nasal polyps, or sinus blockages.
