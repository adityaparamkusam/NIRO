Functional Endoscopic Sinus Surgery: Indications and Recovery
Functional Endoscopic Sinus Surgery is a minimally invasive procedure that helps in treating
chronic sinusitis
,
nasal polyps
, and other sinus-related issues. This surgery aims to restore proper sinus function by clearing blockages and promoting better drainage.
If you have been suffering from recurrent
sinus infections
, facial pain, headaches, or
nasal congestion
, Functional Endoscopic Sinus Surgery may be the best treatment option for you.
This surgery is performed using an endoscope, a thin, flexible tube with a camera attached that allows the surgeon to see inside your sinuses without making any external incisions.
Indications of Functional Endoscopic Sinus Surgery
Functional Endoscopic Sinus Surgery is recommended for patients who have not responded well to conservative treatments such as medication and nasal sprays.
Chronic sinusitis that does not improve with medication
Nasal polyps causing breathing difficulties
Recurrent sinus infections
Sinus blockages or structural abnormalities
Chronic facial pain or headaches related to sinus issues
If you are experiencing any of these symptoms, it is essential to consult with an ENT specialist to determine if Functional Endoscopic Sinus Surgery is the right treatment for you.
Steps involved in Functional Endoscopic Sinus Surgery Surgery
Functional Endoscopic Sinus Surgery is typically performed under general anesthesia on an outpatient basis.
The surgeon inserts an endoscope into the nostrils to visualize the sinus cavities.
Small instruments are used to remove any blockages, polyps, or infected tissue from the sinuses.
The surgeon may also widen the natural openings of the sinuses to promote better drainage.
The procedure usually takes about 1-2 hours, depending on the complexity of the case.
After the surgery, you will be monitored in the recovery room before being discharged home the same day. It is essential to follow your surgeon's post-operative instructions carefully to ensure a smooth recovery.
Who will perform Functional Endoscopic Sinus Surgery Surgery?
Functional Endoscopic Sinus Surgery is typically performed by an Otolaryngologist, also known as an ENT (Ear, Nose, and Throat) surgeon.
These specialists have extensive training and experience in diagnosing and treating conditions of the ear, nose, and throat, including sinus issues.
Before undergoing Functional Endoscopic Sinus Surgery, it is essential to choose a skilled and experienced ENT surgeon who can provide comprehensive care and personalized treatment options based on your specific needs.
You can ask your primary care physician for a referral or research local ENT surgeons online to find the right fit for you.
Preparing for Functional Endoscopic Sinus Surgery Surgery
Before undergoing Functional Endoscopic Sinus Surgery, your surgeon will provide you with specific instructions to help you prepare for the procedure.
Avoid taking certain medications like blood thinners before surgery.
Arrange for transportation to and from the surgical facility.
Fast for a certain period before the surgery, as directed by your surgeon.
Inform your surgeon of any allergies or medical conditions you have.
It is essential to communicate openly with your surgeon and ask any questions you may have about the surgery or recovery process. Being well-prepared can help ensure a successful outcome and a smoother recovery.
Recovery after Functional Endoscopic Sinus Surgery surgery
The recovery period after Functional Endoscopic Sinus Surgery surgery varies from patient to patient but typically involves minimal discomfort and a quick return to normal activities.
Rest and avoid strenuous activities for the first few days after surgery.
Use saline nasal sprays or rinses to keep the nasal passages moist and promote healing.
Attend follow-up appointments with your surgeon to monitor your progress.
Avoid blowing your nose forcefully or engaging in activities that could increase pressure in the sinuses.
Most patients experience significant improvement in their symptoms following Functional Endoscopic Sinus Surgery surgery and can enjoy long-lasting relief from chronic sinus issues.
If you have any concerns or experience unexpected symptoms during your recovery, do not hesitate to contact your surgeon for guidance.
Before and After Functional Endoscopic Sinus Surgery surgery
Before undergoing Functional Endoscopic Sinus Surgery surgery, it is essential to have a thorough consultation with your ENT surgeon to discuss your symptoms, medical history, and treatment options.
Your surgeon will perform a physical examination and may order imaging tests to evaluate the extent of your sinus issues.
After Functional Endoscopic Sinus Surgery surgery, you can expect to experience some mild discomfort, nasal congestion, and drainage in the days following the procedure.
These symptoms are normal and typically resolve within a week or two as the sinuses heal and inflammation subsides.
It is essential to follow your surgeon's post-operative instructions carefully, including taking prescribed medications, attending follow-up appointments, and avoiding certain activities during the recovery period.
By following these guidelines, you can optimize your recovery and achieve the best possible outcome from Functional Endoscopic Sinus Surgery surgery.

Frequently Asked Questions
What is functional endoscopic sinus surgery (FESS)?
Functional endoscopic sinus surgery (FESS) is a minimally invasive procedure that uses a thin, lighted tube with a camera to help surgeons remove blockages and improve sinus drainage, reducing inflammation and infection.
What conditions are treated with functional endoscopic sinus surgery?
Functional endoscopic sinus surgery is used to treat chronic sinusitis, nasal polyps, sinus tumors, and structural issues within the sinuses that cause breathing problems.
How does FESS differ from traditional sinus surgery?
Functional endoscopic sinus surgery (FESS) is less invasive than traditional sinus surgery. FESS uses an endoscope to see and remove blockages in the sinuses, leading to a quicker recovery and less postoperative pain compared to traditional surgery.
How long is recovery after functional endoscopic sinus surgery?
Recovery after functional endoscopic sinus surgery typically takes about 1 to 2 weeks. You may experience some discomfort and nasal congestion during this time, but most people can return to normal activities within a few weeks.
Is functional endoscopic sinus surgery a common procedure?
Yes, functional endoscopic sinus surgery is a common procedure used to treat chronic sinusitis and nasal polyps that do not respond to other treatments.
What are the risks of functional endoscopic sinus surgery?
The risks of functional endoscopic sinus surgery include bleeding, infection, damage to surrounding structures, and a rare chance of cerebrospinal fluid leak.
How successful is functional endoscopic sinus surgery?
Functional endoscopic sinus surgery is a safe and effective procedure that can improve symptoms in around 80-90% of patients with chronic sinusitis.
What should I expect during and after functional endoscopic sinus surgery?
During functional endoscopic sinus surgery, you will be under general anesthesia. After the surgery, you may experience some nasal congestion, mild discomfort, and nasal drainage for a few days.
Does FESS improve breathing?
Yes, FESS (Functional Endoscopic Sinus Surgery) can improve breathing by clearing blocked sinuses and improving airflow.
Can FESS be done on both sides of the sinuses?
Yes, Functional Endoscopic Sinus Surgery (FESS) can be done on both sides of the sinuses if there is a need to treat sinus issues on both sides.
