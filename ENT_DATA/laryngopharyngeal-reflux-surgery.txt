Laryngopharyngeal Reflux Surgery: Indications and
Laryngopharyngeal reflux, also known as acid reflux, can be a challenging condition to manage. When lifestyle changes and medications are not effective in controlling symptoms like throat burning and acid regurgitation, surgery may be considered as a treatment option.
Laryngopharyngeal reflux surgery aims to strengthen the lower esophageal sphincter to prevent stomach acid from flowing back up into the throat.
The best treatment for laryngopharyngeal reflux surgery involves a comprehensive approach that addresses the underlying causes of acid reflux. This may include dietary modifications, weight management, and stress reduction techniques in addition to the surgical procedure.
Indications of Laryngopharyngeal Reflux Surgery
Laryngopharyngeal reflux surgery is typically recommended for individuals who have not responded well to conservative treatments such as medications and lifestyle modifications.
Persistent throat burning or irritation despite medication
Recurrent acid regurgitation
Complications of acid reflux such as Barrett's esophagus
Desire to discontinue long-term medication use
Steps involved in Laryngopharyngeal Reflux Surgery Surgery
Laryngopharyngeal reflux surgery is usually performed using minimally invasive techniques such as laparoscopy.
Making small incisions in the abdomen
Inserting a laparoscope and other surgical instruments
Strengthening the lower esophageal sphincter
Closing the incisions
The goal of laryngopharyngeal reflux surgery is to reduce the frequency and severity of acid reflux symptoms, such as throat burning and acid regurgitation.
Who will perform Laryngopharyngeal Reflux Surgery Surgery?
Laryngopharyngeal reflux surgery is typically performed by a gastroenterologist or a surgeon specializing in gastrointestinal disorders. These healthcare professionals have the expertise and training to diagnose and treat conditions like acid reflux effectively.
It is essential to choose a healthcare provider who is experienced in performing laryngopharyngeal reflux surgery to ensure the best possible outcome.
Preparing for Laryngopharyngeal Reflux Surgery Surgery
Before undergoing laryngopharyngeal reflux surgery, patients will need to follow specific preparation guidelines provided by their healthcare provider.
Fasting for a certain period before the surgery
Stopping certain medications that may interfere with the procedure
Arranging for transportation to and from the surgical facility
Discussing any concerns or questions with the healthcare team
It is essential to follow these preparation instructions carefully to ensure a smooth and successful surgical experience.
Recovery after Laryngopharyngeal Reflux Surgery surgery
Recovery after laryngopharyngeal reflux surgery typically involves a short hospital stay and a period of rest at home.
Patients may experience some discomfort or soreness in the abdominal area, which can be managed with pain medications prescribed by the healthcare provider.
It is essential to follow post-operative care instructions provided by the surgical team to promote healing and prevent complications.
During the recovery period, patients may need to follow a specific diet to allow the surgical site to heal properly. This may include soft foods and liquids for a certain period before gradually reintroducing solid foods.
It is important to attend follow-up appointments with the healthcare provider to monitor progress and address any concerns.
Before and After Laryngopharyngeal Reflux Surgery surgery
Before undergoing laryngopharyngeal reflux surgery, it is essential to discuss the potential risks and benefits of the procedure with the healthcare provider. This will help set realistic expectations and ensure that the decision to proceed with surgery is well-informed.
After laryngopharyngeal reflux surgery, patients may experience an improvement in symptoms such as throat burning and acid regurgitation. It is important to continue following a healthy lifestyle and dietary habits to maintain the benefits of the surgical procedure.
Regular follow-up appointments with the healthcare provider are essential to monitor progress and address any issues that may arise.
Overall, laryngopharyngeal reflux surgery can be an effective treatment option for individuals who have not responded well to conservative treatments.
By working closely with a healthcare provider and following post-operative care instructions, patients can achieve relief from acid reflux symptoms and improve their quality of life.

Frequently Asked Questions
What is laryngopharyngeal reflux surgery?
Laryngopharyngeal reflux surgery is a procedure to treat severe cases of acid reflux that have not responded to other treatments. It aims to strengthen the lower esophageal sphincter to prevent stomach acid from flowing back into the throat.
When is laryngopharyngeal reflux surgery necessary?
Laryngopharyngeal reflux surgery is typically considered when medications and lifestyle changes have not improved symptoms or if there are complications like ulcers, strictures, or Barrett's esophagus.
How is laryngopharyngeal reflux surgery performed?
Laryngopharyngeal reflux surgery is usually done using a minimally invasive procedure called laparoscopic fundoplication. In this surgery, the top of the stomach is wrapped around the lower esophagus to strengthen the valve between the esophagus and the stomach.
What are the risks of laryngopharyngeal reflux surgery?
Risks of laryngopharyngeal reflux surgery may include bleeding, infection, and complications related to anesthesia. It's essential to discuss these risks with your healthcare provider before the procedure.
How long does it take to recover from laryngopharyngeal reflux surgery?
Recovery time after laryngopharyngeal reflux surgery varies, but most people can expect to start feeling better within a few weeks to a couple of months after the procedure.
Does laryngopharyngeal reflux surgery improve symptoms of reflux?
Laryngopharyngeal reflux surgery can help improve symptoms of reflux when lifestyle changes and medications haven't been effective.
Can laryngopharyngeal reflux surgery prevent long-term damage?
Laryngopharyngeal reflux surgery can help manage symptoms and prevent long-term damage like chronic cough, hoarseness, and throat irritation.
Is laryngopharyngeal reflux surgery a permanent solution?
Laryngopharyngeal reflux surgery can provide long-term relief for some people, but it may not be a permanent solution for everyone.
What lifestyle changes should be followed after laryngopharyngeal reflux surgery?
After laryngopharyngeal reflux surgery, it's important to maintain a healthy diet, avoid eating late at night, elevate your head while sleeping, and avoid triggers like caffeine, alcohol, and spicy foods to help prevent reflux symptoms.
How successful is laryngopharyngeal reflux surgery?
Laryngopharyngeal reflux surgery can be successful in relieving symptoms and preventing complications in some patients, but success rates can vary. It is important to discuss the potential outcomes and risks with your healthcare provider.
